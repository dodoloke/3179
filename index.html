Yearly Average Surface Temperature (°C) — 2022

Global patterns of surface temperature in 2022 reveal a warm planet. Regions across Africa, Northern South America and Southeast Asia remain the hottest, while higher latitudes cool towards the poles. Next, Europe and China show elevated reds reflecting the record summer heatwaves and Australia and Southern Africa also sit in the warm band. Cooler tones persist over Northen Canada, Siberia and the Himalayas. The map underscores a persistent gradient: heat concentrated in the tropics, with recent intensification in populated mid-latitudes.

What to look for

These modelled estimates (2020—2039) show warmer annual means clustering along the northern interior and parts of Queensland. Hover for station details and compare annual vs seasonal views to spot regional gradients.

Notice coast vs inland contrasts, stations near mountain ranges, and any outliers that don't match nearby sites. The colour scale is sequential: darker means warmer.

Note: These are climate estimates, not live readings. Results depend on the underlying model/ensemble.

Rainfall vs Max Temperature (°C)

What to look for

The chart plots daily max temperature against rainfall. Most rain happens around 15—30 °C, but heavy falls can still occur on cooler or warmer days. This shows that temperature alone doesn't explain heavy rain.

Filter by year to compare patterns. Look for clusters (different weather types) and a wider spread at higher temps (more variation on hot days).
